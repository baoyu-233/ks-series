# Staged RPG Roadmap

## Stage 0: Stable Survival Foundation

Goal: existing survival, economy, land, storage, and trade loops are understandable and reliable.

Exit criteria:

- New players can earn, store, trade, and build without RPG knowledge.
- Official pricing, taxes, and sinks are monitored before adding high-value RPG rewards.
- Core economy and anti-exploit paths have operational verification.

## Stage 1: Light RPG Discovery

Goal: give ordinary survival play a reason to explore and specialize.

Foundation work: introduce `ks-RPG`, migrate existing `ks-Skill` grants, and expose a stable combat-proof
API before assigning high-value dungeon rewards. Keep MMOProfiles uninstalled; configure MMOCore as a
single shared starter identity with no duplicate party/guild or core active-skill ownership, use MMOItems
for a small fixed equipment catalog, and restrict MMOInventory to two rings plus one amulet.

- Begin with a "relic prelude": introduce a small set of profession-aligned materials and recipes, but do
  not award general RPG XP for ordinary harvesting or machine automation.
- Add visible field elites, special resource nodes, and short solo/small-group contracts in existing biomes.
- Release one equipment upgrade path: its base rank uses coins and survival materials, while its defining
  breakthrough requires a field-elite combat mark.
- Use account-bound cooperation marks as the first proof of mastery. Start instancing at one concurrent,
  four-player dungeon and verify MSPT, heap, and GC behavior before raising the cap.
- Add an in-game codex or quest board only after the loops exist.

## Stage 2: Cooperative Challenge

Goal: establish repeatable group play without making it mandatory for survival progression.

- Add one themed dungeon or world event with a clear preparation loop.
- Release the Frostbound Conductor only after its weapon adaptation and core mechanics pass live tests.
- Reward upgrade ingredients, cosmetics, access materials, and account-bound clear seals; limit finished-gear drops.
- Track completion, failure, time-to-clear, and reward supply before adding another boss.

## Stage 3: Regional Identity

Goal: make different areas of the survival world desirable for distinct reasons.

- Give regions signature resources, elites, contracts, and local crafting relationships.
- Connect enterprises and real estate to community projects or logistics, not combat power purchase.
- Add rotating events sparingly, with predictable schedules and economy caps.

## Stage 4: Long-Term Seasons

Goal: create renewal without wiping player identity.

- Use seasonal objectives, cosmetics, new recipes, and temporary regional events.
- Preserve homes, core economy history, and durable player achievements unless a reset is explicitly designed.
- Retire or rebalance outdated reward sources before introducing a stronger tier.
